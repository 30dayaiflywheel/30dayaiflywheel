Opticians – AI Playbook

Increase eye exam bookings, lift dispense conversion, and speed collections

What this page gives you

This playbook shows where AI helps an optician today. It focuses on full diaries, higher glasses and contact lens sales, and smooth handover from test to dispense.

Use the 30 Day AI Flywheel: Identify Cycle Measure Scale.

Typical Growth Drivers

  • Fill the diary with eye exams and contact lens check ups.
  • Increase dispense conversion from test to glasses or contacts.
  • Lift average order value with lens options and a second pair.
  • Reduce no shows and speed collections when orders arrive.

AI Use Cases Already Working

  • Booking assistant for tests, check ups, and frame questions on web and WhatsApp.
  • Smart recalls and reminders by SMS and email based on last test date.
  • Lens recommendation helper single vision vs varifocal, coatings, blue light, thinner lenses.
  • Virtual try on prompts for shortlisted frames.
  • Lab status updates with ready to collect messages and follow ups.

Data Readiness (What you need)

  • Appointment history with test dates, type, and outcome.
  • Retail POS by frame, lens, coating, and price.
  • Lab order statuses ordered, in lab, shipped, ready to collect.
  • Patient CRM with consent flags and contact details.

CSV exports are fine to start. Keep health data private and use the minimum needed for each step.

Metrics to Track ROI

  • Diary fill rate and no show rate.
  • Dispense conversion test to purchase.
  • Average order value lenses and add ons.
  • Second pair attach rate including sunglasses.
  • Turnaround time order to collection and remake rate.

Example 30 Day Focus

  • Week 1: add a booking assistant and turn on reminders and recalls for due and overdue patients.
  • Week 2: enable a simple lens recommender and add prompts for coatings and blue light where relevant.
  • Week 3: set a second pair offer sunglasses or computer pair for seven days after the test and add virtual try on to the follow up.
  • Week 4: connect lab order statuses and send ready to collect messages. Review fill rate, conversion, average order value, and remake rate vs last month.

What “good” looks like

  • Diary fill rate above 85 percent with fewer no shows.
  • Dispense conversion up by 5 to 12 points.
  • Average order value up by £10 to £30 from lens options.
  • Second pair attach rate rising week by week.
  • Faster collections and fewer remakes.

Keep it simple. One change per week. Measure, then scale what works.

Set up in under an hour

  • Export last 12 months of appointments, POS, and lab orders to CSV.
  • Switch on confirmations and reminders for bookings and collections.
  • Add the assistant to your site and WhatsApp with clear CTAs.

If you use Optix, Ocuco, or a PMS plus POS combo, start with built in reports and message tools.

Fast wins to test

  • Second pair message 20 percent off sunglasses within seven days of test.
  • Blue light and anti reflective prompt for screen heavy patients.
  • Ready to collect message with a link to book a fitting slot.

Guardrails

  • Do not give clinical advice by message. Offer a quick call with the optometrist for concerns.
  • Keep patient data private and limit who can access it.
  • Get consent for messages and make opt out easy.
  • Keep records of advice given in store where required.
