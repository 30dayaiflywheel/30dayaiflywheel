Pharmacies AI Playbook

Grow services improve repeat adherence cut wait times and reduce phone load

What this page gives you

This playbook shows where AI helps a community pharmacy today. It focuses on more booked services, stronger repeat adherence, smoother queues, and quick answers that keep the phone quiet.

Use the 30 Day AI Flywheel: Identify Cycle Measure Scale.

Typical Growth Drivers

  • Increase bookings for flu jabs travel jabs blood pressure checks contraception service and new medicine support.
  • Lift repeat pick up rates and reduce uncollected bags.
  • Shorten queue time at peak and lower walk aways.
  • Reduce phone calls on when will my prescription be ready and opening times.
  • Improve over the counter add on sales with safe prompts.

AI Use Cases Already Working

  • Service booking assistant for flu travel and blood pressure with reminders and easy reschedule.
  • Repeat ready alerts with slot pick up windows and delivery scheduling.
  • Simple symptom guidance that signposts to services or GP out of hours where needed.
  • Stock and space tips for cold and flu allergy pain relief and baby aisle based on season and local demand.
  • Call deflection answers for ID needed EPS status opening hours and delivery zones.
  • Exception alerts for bags over seven days and fridge line expiry.

Data Readiness What you need

  • PMR exports or reports with repeats due collected and not collected.
  • Service logs for flu blood pressure contraception and travel with dates and outcomes.
  • Stock list with on hand expiry for fridge and high value lines.
  • Contact list with consent flags for SMS email or WhatsApp.
  • Basic rota or at least footfall by hour and day.

Start with CSV exports. Keep patient data private and only use what you need for each step.

Metrics to Track ROI

  • Service bookings per day show up rate and rebook rate.
  • Repeat adherence on time pick up rate and bags uncollected over seven days.
  • Queue time at peak and walk aways.
  • Calls answered and average wait time.
  • OTC attach rate to services and repeats.
  • Waste on fridge lines close to expiry.

Example 30 Day Focus

  • Week 1: add a service booking assistant for flu and blood pressure with SMS reminders. Add a simple FAQ for opening hours EPS status ID and delivery.
  • Week 2: load last 12 months of repeats and service data. Turn on repeat ready alerts with slot pick up windows.
  • Week 3: set up safe OTC prompts linked to booked services for example pain relief with flu jab after the jab is done.
  • Week 4: review show ups repeat adherence queue time calls and OTC attach rate versus last month. Keep the best two wins.

What good looks like

  • Show ups for booked services up by 10 to 20 points.
  • Bags uncollected over seven days down by 25 to 40 percent.
  • Average phone wait time down by 30 to 50 percent.
  • OTC attach rate up by 5 to 10 points on key services.
  • Fewer fridge line write offs through expiry alerts.

Keep it simple. One change per week. Measure then scale what works.

Set up in under an hour

  • Export repeats service logs and stock to CSV.
  • Switch on confirmations and reminders for bookings and collections.
  • Add the assistant to your site with clear CTAs and a QR at the counter.

If you use ProScript Connect RxWeb Titan Pharmacy Manager or similar start with built in reports and message tools.

Fast wins to test

  • Flu jab booking link on your Google Business profile and site header.
  • Repeat ready alerts with two hour pick up windows to spread footfall.
  • Quiet hour texts for blood pressure checks and contraception service.
  • Fridge line expiry alerts five days out with pre made labels.

Guardrails

  • Don’t give clinical advice by message. Offer a call back with a pharmacist for concerns.
  • Follow your PGDs SOPs and local pathways. Keep full records.
  • Keep patient data private and role based. Get consent and make opt out easy.
  • Document advice given where your regulator or insurer requires it.

Integration Pathways

  • PMR and EPS: reports for repeats due collected and not collected.
  • Messaging: WhatsApp Business SMS and email for reminders and updates.
  • Payments: links for private services and delivery fees.
  • Referrals: simple intake forms for CPCS and blood pressure checks.

Adopt step by step. You won’t need to replace your PMR to start.

AI Build Options

  • Low code: n8n or Zapier for reminders repeat alerts and routing.
  • Chat layer: a safe assistant that uses approved answers and hands off to a human when needed.
  • Forecasts: simple season and day models for flu jab stock and staff rotas.
  • Dashboards: Google Sheets Looker Studio or Power BI for weekly review.

Keep messages short clear and linked to one action.

Staff Workflow Impact

  • Fewer where is my prescription calls.
  • Better rotas around service bookings and repeats due.
  • Safer OTC prompts after a service reduces awkward upsell moments.
  • Less rush on the fridge thanks to expiry alerts and batch checks.

Cost vs Return

  • +6 paid services per day @ £15 net is about £2,700 per month.
  • Uncollected bags down by 30 percent reduces waste and rework time.
  • Phone time down 40 percent frees staff hours for services.

Most tools cost far less than the gains above.

Patient Experience Gains

  • Clear booking and reminders so people don’t forget.
  • Shorter waits at peak with better slotting.
  • Simple answers online for ID EPS status opening hours and deliveries.
  • Follow ups that help people use medicines correctly.

Pharmacy Readiness Checklist

  • PMR exports for repeats and service logs.
  • Contact list with consent flags and segments.
  • WhatsApp Business or an SMS sender set up.
  • Named owner for a weekly results review.

If you tick most of these you can start this week.

Future Plays 12 to 24 months

  • Voice capture for service notes to speed up documentation.
  • Demand forecasting for flu jab stock and rota planning.
  • Personalised reminder timing based on adherence patterns.
  • Computer vision expiry checks for fridge items.

Case Example

  • High street pharmacy with two pharmacists.
  • Turned on service booking assistant repeat alerts and FAQ.
  • Show ups up 16 points for flu jabs in four weeks.
  • Calls down 45 percent on EPS and opening time queries.
  • Bags over seven days dropped by a third.

Results will vary. The weekly review locks in wins and drops weak ideas.
